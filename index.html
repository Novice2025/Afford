Afford: Capacidade de Pagar/Proporcionar (Afford: Ability to Pay/Provide)

Significado e Uso (Meaning and Usage)

Como verbo, **afford** significa **ter condições financeiras para pagar algo, poder arcar com o custo de algo, ou proporcionar algo (como uma oportunidade ou benefício)**. (As a verb, **afford** means **to have the financial means to pay for something, to be able to bear the cost of something, or to provide something (such as an opportunity or benefit)**.) É frequentemente usado com os modais "can" ou "cannot". (It is often used with the modals "can" or "cannot".)

  • "We cannot **afford** to lose this client."
    (Não podemos nos **dar ao luxo** de perder este cliente.)
  • "The company can **afford** to invest in new technology."
    (A empresa pode **arcar** com o investimento em nova tecnologia.)
  • "Can we **afford** to hire more staff right now?"
    (Podemos **ter condições** de contratar mais funcionários agora?)
  • "This new system will **afford** us greater efficiency."
    (Este novo sistema nos **proporcionará** maior eficiência.)
  • "They could not **afford** the high cost of living in the city center."
    (Eles não podiam **arcar** com o alto custo de vida no centro da cidade.)

Afford: Why, How, and When to Use

Why use "Afford"?

Using "afford" allows for precise communication regarding financial capability, resource allocation, and the feasibility of actions or purchases. It conveys whether something is within one's means without causing undue strain. In a professional context, it's crucial for discussions about budgets, investments, and operational capacity, indicating a thoughtful assessment of resources.

How to use "Afford"?

  • As a verb: "Afford" is always a verb. It is most commonly used with modal verbs like "can" or "cannot" (or "could" / "could not"). For example: "We can afford to upgrade our software." (Podemos nos dar ao luxo de atualizar nosso software.)
  • It can also be used to mean "to provide" or "to grant," as in: "The new policy will afford employees more flexibility." (A nova política proporcionará aos funcionários mais flexibilidade.)

When to use "Afford"?

  • When discussing budgets, expenses, and financial planning in business meetings.
  • To evaluate the feasibility of new projects, hiring, or investments.
  • When talking about the capacity to absorb costs or risks without negative impact.
  • In personal finance discussions, such as buying a home or a car.
  • When describing what a situation or opportunity can provide.

Difference between "Afford" and "Can Buy / Have Enough Money"

While "can buy" or "have enough money" focus simply on the immediate availability of funds for a transaction, "afford" implies a deeper assessment of financial capacity and sustainability. "Can buy" means you have the cash at hand. "Afford" means you can make the purchase or bear the cost without jeopardizing your overall financial well-being or other commitments.

For example, you might "have enough money" to buy a very expensive car, but you might not "afford" it if doing so would deplete your savings, prevent you from paying bills, or lead to long-term financial instability. "Afford" considers the broader financial picture and the long-term implications of a cost or commitment.

  • Can Buy / Have Enough Money: Focuses on immediate funds for a transaction. (Ex: "I have enough money to buy this coffee.")
  • Afford: Implies financial capacity and sustainability, considering the broader impact on resources. (Ex: "I can afford this coffee without affecting my budget.")
